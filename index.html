Durham University: Natural Sciences Masters with Dual Honours Biology and Chemistry (2012-2016)

My master’s project was part of an established research group of several academics and students from across Chemistry, Physics and Biosciences, and a company, High Force Research Ltd. The focus was ‘fluorescent synthetic retinoids’ which were synthesised as part of a Chemistry PhD student’s project. The aim of my project was to understand these compounds’ potential for use as probes and confirm comparable action to the endogenous compound ATRA in vitro, using qPCR and fluorescent microscopy. In doing so, I discovered that at certain wavelengths of light the compounds triggered extremely targeted cell death. This technology was further developed after my research and has resulted in a spin-off company called Light-Ox, which gave me an early insight into the importance of IP to research. I found this an exciting collaborative project with lots of people with which to exchange ideas. Other fourth year modules included bio-molecular analysis, bioactive chemistry, and research skills. Official university result: Upper Second-Class Honours.
